About our UTM Builder Tool

UTM stands for Urchin Tracking Module. It is used to track your campaign URLs by Google Analytics and many other analytics softwares.

Our UTM Campaign Builder Tool allows you to easily build UTM tracking codes to track your campaign performance in Google Analytics or in any other analytics software.

If you are running multiple campaigns, then you can easily generate unique links to track the performance of each campaign.

UTM parameters & its types

UTM parameters are tags that we add to a specific URL so that when our link is clicked by the users, then the tags are sent back to Google Analytics for tracking purposes.

There are 5 types of UTM parameters:

  1. Campaign Source (utm_source)
  2. Campaign Medium (utm_medium)
  3. Campaign Name (utm_campaign)
  4. Campaign Term (utm_term)
  5. Campaign Content (utm_content)
Campaign Parameters Usage with Example
Campaign Source (utm_source) Used to identify the user's source such as search engine, social media platform, newsletter name, or other source.
Example: Google, Facebook, Instagram etc
Campaign Medium (utm_medium) Used to identify the user's medium such as email, social media or cost-per-click.
Example: side_banner, email etc
Campaign Name (utm_campaign) Used to identify the name of the Campaign for a specific product promotion or season offer.
Example: diwali_sale, new_year_offer etc
Campaign Term (utm_term) Used to identify the user's term/keyword for paid search of a specific ad.
Example: utm+builder, online+tool etc
Campaign Content (utm_content) Used to identify the A/B testing and content-targeted ads.
Example: logo_link, text_link etc
